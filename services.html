Dental implants & full-mouth reconstruction

The closest thing to growing your tooth back.

A dental implant isn't something you replace in a decade — it's a titanium root, fused directly to your jawbone, engineered to function for the rest of your life. That permanence is exactly why the investment looks different from a bridge or a denture: you're not paying for a temporary fix you'll redo every five to ten years. You're paying once for a foundation that doesn't need to be relined, replaced, or revisited.

It's also why the cheapest implant quote you find isn't necessarily the best value. Lower-cost providers often skip the 3D surgical guide, use generic off-brand fixtures instead of medical-grade titanium, or hand off your case to whoever's in the chair that day. Every fee at Lucerne Dental Atelier includes the planning, materials, and continuity of care that determine whether your implant lasts five years or fifty.

Which one fits your situation?

From one missing tooth to a complete new bite.

Every case is different, and your treatment coordinator will recommend the right path during your consultation. Here's what each option actually involves.

Single-Tooth Implant

Replaces one missing tooth without grinding down the healthy teeth on either side, the way a traditional bridge requires. The implant stands entirely on its own, preserving the bone and structure around it.

  • Neighboring teeth left completely untouched
  • Single in-office surgical visit, typically under 60 minutes
  • Final crown placed after 3–4 months of healing

For two or more adjacent missing teeth, a bridge anchored by implants on either end replaces the entire span — without relying on your remaining natural teeth to carry the load, the way a traditional tooth-supported bridge does.

How it actually happens

Four stages, fully mapped before we begin.

1

Consultation & 3D-Guided Planning

A cone beam CT scan maps your jawbone and nerve pathways in three dimensions, letting us plan exact implant position and angle on screen before any drilling — eliminating exploratory guesswork.

2

Guided Implant Placement

Using a custom 3D-printed surgical guide built from your scan, the implant is placed exactly where it was planned. Sedation options are matched to your comfort level, not a one-size-fits-all default.

3

Healing & Osseointegration

Over roughly three to four months, the implant fuses directly with your jawbone. A temporary restoration keeps your smile complete while we monitor healing at regular check-ins.

4

Custom Final Restoration

Your permanent crown, bridge, or full-arch prosthesis is fabricated, shade-matched, and bonded into place — then bite-checked and polished until it feels indistinguishable from a natural tooth.

Am I actually a candidate for implants?

Most patients are, even those who were told years ago they weren't. Adequate bone density is the main requirement, and if you've lost bone volume, a bone graft or sinus lift can often rebuild the foundation needed. Your 3D scan tells us definitively during your consultation.

How uncomfortable is the placement procedure?

Most patients describe it as easier than they expected — comparable to a tooth extraction. With local anesthesia and, if you choose, sedation, discomfort during the procedure is minimal, and any post-op soreness is typically managed with over-the-counter medication for a few days.

Does dental insurance cover implants?

Coverage varies widely by plan — some cover a portion of the crown but not the surgical placement, others cover more. Our team verifies your specific benefits before treatment starts and combines insurance with financing to minimize your out-of-pocket cost. See our financing page for details.

Porcelain veneers & smile design

Half a millimeter of porcelain, engineered to look like you were just born this way.

A great veneer doesn't look like a veneer — it looks like an unusually good set of natural teeth. That illusion takes hand-layered porcelain, built tooth-by-tooth by a master ceramist who matches translucency, shape, and shade to your exact facial proportions. It's the difference between a smile that looks "done" and one that looks like yours, only finished.

Mass-produced veneers cut corners in ways that show: bulkier porcelain to save lab time, limited shade options, and shapes pulled from a generic mold rather than designed around your face. Choosing the right material

Three ways to get there, at three different price points.

There's no universally "right" veneer material — only the right one for your teeth, timeline, and budget. We'll recommend a specific path during your digital smile design consultation.

Hand-layered, full-coverage porcelain that mimics the way light passes through real enamel. The gold standard for durability, stain resistance, and a result that holds up for fifteen to twenty years with normal care.

From mockup to mirror

Will my teeth be sensitive after getting veneers?

Mild, temporary sensitivity to hot or cold is common for the first few days as your teeth adjust, especially with porcelain veneers that require minimal enamel removal. It typically resolves on its own within a week and is rarely significant enough to need medication.

Are veneers reversible?

Ultra-thin no-prep veneers are largely reversible since little to no enamel is removed. Traditional porcelain veneers, which require minimal enamel reduction, are considered a permanent cosmetic choice — which is exactly why the diagnostic trial smile step exists, so you can preview the result before anything irreversible happens.

How do I keep my veneers looking new?

Brush and floss exactly as you would with natural teeth, avoid using your teeth as tools (opening packages, biting nails), and wear a nightguard if you grind your teeth. With that routine, most porcelain veneers comfortably reach the high end of their fifteen-to-twenty-year lifespan.
